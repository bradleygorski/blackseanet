About the Black Sea Networks Initiative

The Black Sea Networks is an international teaching and learning initiative at Columbia University. We envision the Black Sea as an open sea with extensive connectivity networks along transportation and trade routes and global information channels.

The wider Black Sea region stretches from the Balkans to the Caucasus and from the Russian and Ukrainian steppes to Anatolia. As a contact zone between Europe, Central Asia, and the Middle East, and the scene of ongoing political and ecologic crises with global repercussions, it constitutes the next frontier of European strategic thinking.

The project builds upon existing initiatives, including the Columbia-Boğaziçi Summer Program in Balkan Transcultural Studies, developed in partnership with Global Centers–Turkey to make Columbia University a leading institution in the burgeoning field of Black Sea Studies. The initiative re-conceptualizes existing programs within a larger Black Sea framework and creates multidisciplinary undergraduate and graduate tracks in “Black Sea Studies” at Columbia’s Department of Slavic Languages.
